# Client Onboarding Playbook

## Purpose

This playbook defines how Revora should onboard a workshop, detailing center, tire shop, fleet maintenance operator, or future service business from discovery through go-live and optimization.

## 1. Discovery

Capture:

- Business model and locations.
- Primary services and revenue lines.
- Existing quote, approval, job, and complaint workflows.
- Current tools such as Excel, WhatsApp, accounting software, DMS, or CRM.
- Customer communication channels.
- Languages used by staff and customers.
- Approval evidence requirements.
- Pain points around trust, documentation, disputes, and response time.

## 2. Data Readiness

Assess whether client data can be imported cleanly:

- File types available.
- Required columns present.
- Duplicates and inconsistent names.
- Customer phone/email coverage.
- Vehicle VIN, plate, make, model, and mileage coverage.
- Branch ownership of records.
- Service catalog consistency.
- Open complaints and unresolved jobs.

## Client Data Pack

Request a client data pack with:

- Branches
- Team/advisors
- Customers
- Vehicles
- Services
- Quotation items
- Complaints
- Documents
- Existing workflows

## 3. Setup

Configure:

- Business profile and branding.
- Branches and working hours.
- Owner and manager access.
- Advisor and staff access through approved invite flow.
- Services and quote presets.
- Default complaint severities and response ownership.
- Customer portal readiness.
- Terms, quote approval language, and document expectations.

## 4. Pilot

Start with one branch or one service team.

Pilot scope:

- New quotes created in Revora.
- Customer approvals tracked through Revora.
- First complaint queue centralized.
- Advisors trained on quote and complaint flows.
- Customer portal tested with a limited customer cohort.

## 5. Staff Training

Training should cover:

- Login and role expectations.
- Customer creation and duplicate prevention.
- Vehicle lookup and service history.
- Quote creation and product transparency.
- Approval status tracking.
- Complaint handling and response etiquette.
- Internal notes vs customer-visible messages.
- Evidence/document upload rules.

## 6. Customer Activation

Activation should focus on customers who have active quotes, jobs, complaints, or upcoming service.

Recommended sequence:

- Link/import customer records.
- Send portal invitation or magic link through approved channels.
- Explain quote approval and complaint tracking benefits.
- Monitor activation rate and support failed logins.
- Avoid mass invites until data quality is verified.

## 7. Go-Live

Go-live checklist:

- Pilot branch validated.
- Owner and managers trained.
- Advisors invited.
- Service catalog reviewed.
- Quote approval flow verified.
- Complaint queue ownership assigned.
- Customer portal activation started.
- Support owner assigned for first week.

## 8. Optimization

After go-live:

- Review quote conversion.
- Review complaint response time.
- Review portal activation.
- Review duplicate customer rate.
- Adjust service templates.
- Improve branch/team permissions.
- Plan multi-branch rollout.

## Common Risks

- Excel inconsistencies.
- WhatsApp approvals without auditable records.
- Duplicate customers.
- Bad phone formats.
- Mixed Arabic/English names.
- Branch ownership confusion.
- Staff resistance.
- Portal adoption.
- Missing VIN/plate data.

## Pilot Success Targets

- 80% new quotes in Revora.
- 70% customers imported.
- 90% advisors invited.
- 20 portal activations.
- First 10 complaints centralized.
- One branch live before multi-branch rollout.

## Operator Notes

Do not promise full automation before data quality is reviewed. A clean pilot is more valuable than a broad import that creates duplicate customers, wrong branch ownership, or unsupported approval history.
